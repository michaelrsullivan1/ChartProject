We're all swimming in a sea of sentiment.

You like to think of yourself as an independent thinker, but you're not. 

Humans predictably make poor decisions.

I know this deeply because I've done it repeatedly.

How did I first come to Bitcoin? By buying the top in 2021.

I'm quite proud of myself for buying MSTR at $17 in 2022... but you know what else I did? 

Bought MSTR over $450.

Honestly kind of impressive.

And what's more... it's quite predictable, I just didn't know it yet.

Let's delve into some data and see just how heavily impacted I was by the euphoric sentiment surrounding me.

I ran every single one of my tweets through sentiment analysis and looked for language indictive of "excitement". I've then mapped this against my baseline tweet and plotted it against the MSTR price. 

Guess what? 

You can quite clearly see that these levels were I was using the most enthusiastic language were specifically around these tops. 

You can literally see how much the market influence my own personal mood.

(chart-1-excitement)

In the early stages of this euphoric period you can see the kind of stuff I was tweeting about the the levels of engagement I was getting around them.

(chart-2-early-excitement)

Then (comically) when the price of Bitcoin "crashed" down to $92k and MSTR was floating right around $300 I posted the following about their being low sentiment... sigh... I was so young and nieve back then.

(chart-3)

I couldn't have been much more wrong about the public sentiment data at the time. 

Let's look at December 29th specifically.

What we are looking at here is the exact same "excitement" data compared to the MSTR and BTC price... with one minor difference.

Instead of it just being MY personal sentiment data, this is an aggregate of all the Bitcoiners posts I've run sentiment analysis on. I've zoomed out a bit further, but you can clearly see that even though MSTR did have a small wick down at the very end of 2024, The overall sentiment data was still really positive. Just look at the period during the summer of 2024 as a contrast. During that long sideways chop, mood was incredibly low.

(chart-4)

Anyone in the Bitcoin treasury market around this time period knows exactly how this felt... but I was there, how could I have been so wrong?

Sentiment.

We are all swimming in a sea of sentiment.

(chart-5-aka-what-is-water)

I keep coming back to this water analogy.

It's so hard to clearly see that which we are clearly surrounded by.

In the same way that the fish don't think about the water that surrounds their entire lives, market participants don't realize that they exist inside of the present sentiment environment and are not immune to it.


Just look at that poor guy from the last tweet... he so confidently thought "sentiment was low". If I could have shown me the same chart that you are looking at now, he likely would have changed his tune.

But we aren't done yet.

I know, I know... we keep ripping on the guy and he was so clearly wrong.

But, alas, we must persevere.

Because past-me has more idiocy that we must learn from (then I promise we'll leave the poor guy alone).

Ok... pop quiz.

When is the worst possible time to buy call options?

Correct (hopefully).

When price is ripping and implied volatility is high.

Ideally, you enter these massive high-upside positions when the price is low and volatility is muted, so when things shift you can benefit disproportionately. 

So naturally... I became an options expert at the exact worst time to buy MSTR call options.

LOL.

Here you can see how many of my tweets were about "options" per week over a period of time.

If you remember the shape of the chart, you'll note that it almost perfectly maps to both of MSTR's price peaks.

(chart-6-options-activity)


In my defense, I write to learn.

Heck. That's exactly what I'm doing right now. Distilling all that I've been (obsessively/autistically) learning about sentiment and sharing it with you so we can both learn from it.

This period of my life was hugely formative for me in that I went through a great deal of financial pain and learned that I was once again getting wrapped up in the turbulent sea that is sentiment. 

People wanted to read my writing about options... not because I was brilliant, but because that's exactly what the market to read about when sentiment is ripping.

I was a symptom of my environment.

As much as I like to think of myself as an independent thinker (which I frankly often am, hell, I wrote a novel because I often have novel ideas to share with the world) I was still completely subjected to the waves of the ocean (market) and pulled out into the riptide.

But this isn't a bad thing.

The only _bad_ thing would have been closing up shop and walking away.

(add-meme-of-diamond-mining-guy)

Today my underlying conviction is just as strong as it was when I first entered the space.

Sure, I did some stupid stuff, but rather than give up and whine about it, I'm actively learning from this rough period (pain is information after all).

I'm using this struggle as an opportunity for growth.

And my personal biggest learning exists around market sentiment.

I'm not immune from it.

I'm not smarter because I spent more time embedded into these communities... in fact the opposite is often true.

The more time you spend around Bitcoiners the more likely you are to be influenced by the current sentiment and narratives that consume our space.

And heck, I'm cool with that.

There is no group of people I respect more and want to spent time interacting with.

BUT... Going forward I'm going to continue to reference the data I have around moods/sentiments so I can check myself (before I wreck myself).

Sentiment waxes and wanes.

Narratives peak and valley.

I'm not a trader. I'm here for the long haul and I believe that Bitcoin is freedom money.

But I want to see the water.

I want to better understand the environment that I exist inside of.

So that's why I'll likely be cautious the next time Peter Schiff is starting to use more "annoyed" language.

(seriously, just look at him end of 2023 into 2024 when it became clear Bitcoin was leaving a bear market... his number of posts with "annoyed" language skyrocketed)

(chart-8-peter-schiff)

And I'll become giga-bullish when he starts rubbing things in about price being down (because he is literally a high-value counter-signal) just look at how we'll you'd have done if you bought when he posted to rub it in and how often he tweets on bottoms.

(chart-9-peter-schiff)

I don't think sentiment analysis is a panacea.

But I do think it's a useful tool in recognizing the state of the market, reducing the likelihood that future me makes the same mistakes that past me stumbled into.

Bitcoin is a mirror.

The protocol is unchanging, but the way humans relate to and develop story around it is constantly shifting.

I'm not a trader, I'm not looking to time tops and I'm sure as hell stacking during the current market conditions, but I want to see more clearly the world we actually exist inside of.

We can't escape from sentiment, but we can get better at recognizing when we are inside of it.
